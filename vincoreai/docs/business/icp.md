# Ideal customer profiles

## ICP 1: HVAC contractor (primary)

**Who:** Owner-operator of a small-to-mid HVAC company. 2–15 technicians. Doing $500K–$5M in annual revenue. Usually the owner is also the sales team.

**Geography:** Canada (Toronto, Calgary, Vancouver), United States (Texas, Florida, midwest)

**Pain points:**
- Emergency service calls come in at all hours — no system to route them to the right technician
- Leads from the website sit in a general email inbox for hours before anyone calls back
- By the time they follow up, the customer has already called a competitor
- No way to tell urgent (broken AC in summer) from routine (annual maintenance) without reading every email
- Owner is too busy doing installs and managing jobs to run sales properly

**Trigger for buying:** Lost a big job recently because of slow follow-up. Competitor is growing faster. Owner is exhausted and knows things need to change.

**Objections:**
- "I'm not tech-savvy" → counter: done-for-you, full documentation, we handle everything
- "How do I know it'll work?" → counter: show the Loom demo of the HVAC blueprint
- "What does it cost?" → counter: one recovered lead per month pays for the system

**Best outreach channel:** Email (website contact), Facebook Messenger (business pages)

**Decision maker:** Owner directly

## ICP 2: Real estate agent / broker (primary)

**Who:** Independent agent or small brokerage with 1–10 agents. Doing 20–100 deals per year. Active on Instagram and LinkedIn.

**Geography:** Canada (Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa)

**Pain points:**
- Leads coming from multiple sources (website, Zillow/Realtor.ca, Instagram DMs, referrals) with no central system
- No way to score or prioritise which leads are serious buyers vs early browsers
- Admin tasks (follow-up emails, document chasing, onboarding new buyer clients) eating into selling time
- CRM either non-existent or unused because it's too complex

**Trigger for buying:** Missed a deal because they forgot to follow up. Colleague is closing more deals with less stress. Team growing and the manual approach is breaking.

**Objections:**
- "I already use [tool]" → counter: we integrate with what you have, we don't replace it
- "I'm too busy to implement this" → counter: done-for-you, minimal time required from you

**Best outreach channel:** Instagram DM, Email

**Decision maker:** Agent directly (individual) or broker principal (team)

---

## ICP 3: Professional services firm (secondary)

**Who:** Small consulting, law, accounting, or advisory firm. 5–50 staff. Project-based work.

**Geography:** Nigeria (Lagos, Abuja), UK, Canada

**Pain points:**
- Proposal process is inconsistent — each consultant does it differently
- Client onboarding has no documented workflow, new clients get different experiences
- Project tracking done in spreadsheets or WhatsApp groups
- Invoice and payment tracking is manual and error-prone

**Trigger for buying:** Lost a client due to poor communication. New project manager joining and wants systems in place. Firm applying for enterprise contracts that require documented processes.

**Best outreach channel:** LinkedIn, Email

**Decision maker:** Managing partner, COO, or Operations Manager

---

## Disqualifying signals (who NOT to pursue)

- Businesses with fewer than 5 inbound leads per month (not enough volume to justify automation)
- Solopreneurs who want to DIY — they want templates, not done-for-you
- Anyone who leads with "make it cheap" before understanding what they're buying
- Companies with no existing digital presence (no website, no CRM, nothing) — too much groundwork before automation is viable
