# VAI-ADM-003 Target Customers and Ideal Client Profiles
**Version:** 1.0 | **Owner:** Opeyemi Idonuagbe | **Status:** Active

---

## Target Markets

**Industries:** HVAC contractors, real estate agents and brokerages, consulting firms, engineering companies, construction and trades, recruitment agencies, legal services, financial services practices, property management, shortlet businesses

**Geographies:** Nigeria, Canada, United States, United Kingdom, Australia, Europe

---

## ICP 1: HVAC Contractor (Primary)

**Who:** Owner-operator of a small-to-mid HVAC company. 2–15 technicians. $500K–$5M annual revenue. Owner is usually also the sales team.

**Geography:** Canada (Toronto, Calgary, Vancouver), United States (Texas, Florida, Midwest)

**Pain points:**
- Emergency calls come in at all hours with no routing system
- Leads sit in a general inbox for hours before anyone calls back
- By the time they follow up, the customer has called a competitor
- No way to distinguish urgent from routine without reading every email
- Owner too busy doing installs to run sales properly

**Trigger for buying:** Lost a big job to slow follow-up. Competitor is growing faster. Owner is exhausted.

**Objections:**
- "I'm not tech-savvy" → counter: done-for-you, full documentation
- "How do I know it'll work?" → counter: Loom demo of working system
- "What does it cost?" → counter: one recovered lead per month pays for it

**Best outreach channel:** Email (website), Facebook Messenger

**Decision maker:** Owner directly

---

## ICP 2: Real Estate Agent / Broker (Primary)

**Who:** Independent agent or small brokerage. 1–10 agents. 20–100 deals per year. Active on Instagram and LinkedIn.

**Geography:** Canada (Toronto, Vancouver, Calgary, Ottawa)

**Pain points:**
- Leads from multiple sources with no central system
- No way to score or prioritise serious buyers vs browsers
- Admin tasks eating into selling time
- CRM non-existent or unused

**Trigger for buying:** Missed a deal from forgotten follow-up. Team growing and manual approach is breaking.

**Best outreach channel:** Instagram DM, Email

**Decision maker:** Agent directly or broker principal

---

## ICP 3: Professional Services Firm (Secondary)

**Who:** Small consulting, law, accounting, or advisory firm. 5–50 staff. Project-based work.

**Geography:** Nigeria (Lagos, Abuja), UK, Canada

**Pain points:**
- Inconsistent proposal process
- No documented client onboarding workflow
- Project tracking in spreadsheets or WhatsApp
- Manual invoice and payment tracking

**Trigger for buying:** Lost a client to poor communication. Applying for enterprise contracts requiring documented processes.

**Best outreach channel:** LinkedIn, Email

**Decision maker:** Managing partner, COO, or Operations Manager

---

## Disqualifying Signals

- Fewer than 5 inbound leads per month
- Solopreneurs who want to DIY
- Anyone who leads with "make it cheap" before understanding the value
- No existing digital presence whatsoever
